Medicare Parts Overview

Part A – Hospital Insurance

Part A helps cover inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice care, and some home health services.

Part B – Medical Insurance

Part B covers doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services, and medical supplies.

Part C – Medicare Advantage

Part C is an alternative to Original Medicare that bundles Part A, Part B, and often Part D coverage through private plans.

Part D – Prescription Drug Coverage

Part D offers coverage for prescription medications, with varying formularies, tiers, and pharmacy networks.

Medigap – Supplemental Coverage

Medigap policies fill the gaps left by Original Medicare, covering copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have both Medicare Advantage and Medigap?
No. You must choose one; having both would duplicate coverage and may result in penalties.
Is there a cost for the consultation?
Our initial consultation is completely free, with no obligation to purchase any plan.
How do I know which prescription drug plan is best?
Compare formularies, tier pricing, and pharmacy networks; our Part D guide walks you through each factor.
Can I change my plan during the year?
Yes, during the Open Enrollment Period (Oct 15‑Dec 7) you can switch or drop plans without penalty.
What if I have a qualifying life event?
A qualifying event (e.g., loss of employer coverage) triggers a Special Enrollment Period allowing plan changes.
Do I need to pay for the brochures?
All downloadable brochures are free and can be accessed via the buttons below.
Is there a cost for Part C (Advantage) plans?
Many Advantage plans have $0 premiums, but you may still pay copays, deductibles, and other out‑of‑pocket costs.
How often should I review my coverage?
Review annually during Open Enrollment or sooner if your health needs change.

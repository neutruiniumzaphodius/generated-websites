Medicare Offerings

Part A – Hospital Insurance

Coverage for inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice, and some home health services.

Benefits include reduced costs for hospital stays, no monthly premium for most beneficiaries, and protection against high out‑of‑pocket expenses.

Part B – Medical Insurance

Covers doctors’ services, outpatient care, preventive services, and medical supplies.

Typical monthly premium is low; you can also receive preventive services at no cost.

Part C – Medicare Advantage

All‑in‑one plan that includes Part A, Part B, and usually Part D, plus extra benefits like vision and dental.

Often lower out‑of‑pocket costs and added services; many plans include prescription drug coverage.

Part D – Prescription Drug Plans

Helps cover the cost of prescription medicines.

Select a plan based on formulary, tier pricing, and pharmacy network to maximise savings.

Medigap – Supplemental Insurance

Fills the “gaps” left by Original Medicare (Part A & B) for deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance.

Offers predictable out‑of‑pocket costs; you can keep your Part D coverage.

Medicare Advantage vs. Medigap

Feature Medicare Advantage (Part C) Medigap (Supplemental)
Monthly Premium Often $0‑$30 (depends on plan) Typically $50‑$200 (varies by carrier)
Out‑of‑Pocket Maximum Set by plan (usually $3,000‑$5,000) No OOP max – you pay deductibles & copays
Prescription Drug Coverage Included in most plans Separate Part D required
Additional Benefits Dental, vision, hearing, gym memberships None (focuses on medical cost coverage)
Network Restrictions Provider network (HMO/PPO) Freedom to see any Medicare‑accepting doctor

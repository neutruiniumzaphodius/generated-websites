Understanding the Medicare System

Medicare is divided into four main parts that work together to provide hospital, medical, and prescription drug coverage. Below you’ll find a clear breakdown of each part and how they fit into a complete Medicare plan.

Part A – Hospital Insurance

What It Covers

In‑patient hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice care, and some home health services.

Key Details

  • Usually $0 premium if you or your spouse paid Medicare taxes.
  • Deductible: $1,600 per benefit period (2024).
  • No out‑of‑pocket maximum for Part A services.

Part B – Medical Insurance

What It Covers

Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services, and medical supplies.

Key Details

  • Standard premium $164.90/month (2024) – may be higher based on income.
  • Annual deductible $233 (2024).
  • Typically 20% coinsurance after deductible.

Part C – Medicare Advantage

What It Covers

All Part A & B benefits plus extra services such as vision, dental, hearing, and fitness programs. Many plans bundle prescription drug coverage (MA‑PDP).

Key Details

  • Premiums vary widely – some $0 plans available.
  • May have lower out‑of‑pocket maximums than Original Medicare.
  • Plan ratings (1‑5 stars) help gauge quality.

Part D – Prescription Drug Coverage

What It Covers

Helps pay for prescription medicines. Plans differ by formulary, tiers, and cost‑sharing.

Key Details

  • Premiums range from $0 to $100+ per month.
  • Coverage gap (“donut hole”) may apply – we help you navigate it.
  • Catastrophic coverage kicks in after reaching out‑of‑pocket threshold.

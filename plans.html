Frequently Asked Questions

Part A covers hospital insurance, including inpatient stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice, and some home health care.

Part B covers medical insurance for doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services, and medical supplies.

Part C, also known as Medicare Advantage, combines Parts A and B (and usually D) into a single private‑plan offering additional benefits.

Part D provides prescription drug coverage, helping to lower medication costs.

Medigap (Medicare Supplement) policies help pay for out‑of‑pocket costs not covered by Original Medicare.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you have a Medicare‑eligible prescription drug need, a Part D plan can help lower your out‑of‑pocket costs. You’re not required to enroll if you have other creditable coverage.

Formularies are lists of covered drugs organized into tiers. Lower‑tier drugs have lower co‑pays. Your plan’s formulary determines which drugs are covered and at what cost.

Plans may require you to use specific pharmacies (network pharmacies) or obtain prior authorization for certain medications. Always verify your plan’s network before filling a prescription.

After you and your plan have spent a combined amount (the “donut hole” threshold), catastrophic coverage kicks in, covering most of the cost of your drugs for the remainder of the year.
